What is the average collection?
The average collection period is the amount of time (measured in days) that it takes for a company to collect and convert it's accounts receivable into cash. It helps in determining the short-term liquidity of a company. How to calculate average collection?
To calculate average collection you should use the following formula:
average collection period = (accounts receivable / net credit sales) * 365 days
example: company x's accounts receivable for the year is $31,000 and the net credit sales is $405,000, calculate the average collection period
using the average collection period formula the answer will be:
average collection period = (accounts receivable / net credit sales) * 365 days
average collection period = ($31,000 / $405,000) * 365
average collection period = 27.93
Why use average collection?
Companies usually use the average collection period in order to make sure that they have enough cash available to meet their financial obligations.
